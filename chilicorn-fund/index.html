Who is it for?

Students and passionate volunteers

If you are part of a student group, or an instructor to one, and looking for a meaningful project to design or implement with our coaching and mentoring, do get in touch!

Contact

Companies and institutions

Any company can get involved by either contributing financially, or providing individuals with the relevant skills to create digital services or solutions. Get in touch with us and we’ll hook you up.

Contact

NGOs, Social Enterprises

If you have a problem worth solving or a project that can change the world, but not enough funding to go for it, apply to be in our program. If your project gets selected, it will be completed pro-bono and you can then take it further.

How does it work?

Its aim is to work with charities to create meaningful and impactful digital services. It seeks to do this by working with partner companies and the Futurice team. We select projects by choosing those that generate value to the individuals working on the project, the company, and society. All three.

Selection criteria

Positive Impact

Is the project making the world better? How much better?
This is the most important measure to consider and it gets double the weight of the others.

Open Source Significance

Is the outcome replicable for other areas and for similar problems? When we share the results as open source, can that bring extra value to the world?

Competence Development

How much relevant learning do participants get out of this project? Are they learning new technologies or better ways of doing things? Can they apply what they learn in future projects?

Student Cooperation Opportunities

Can the project be done together with groups of students, under our guidance? Will they learn relevant things while working on it?

Partner Sponsorship Opportunities

Can our partners contribute to this project either financially, by providing people into the project team, or perhaps by some other means?

PR Opportunities

Could Futurice and any sponsoring partners get well-deserved publicity for doing this project pro bono? Will we be proud of it?

Selection process

1. Collecting applications

When we open applications for the Chilicorn Fund, organisations and institutions can submit their project using a web form provided by us. We will go through the applications and make inquiries, if we find some relevant information missing. Please familiarise yourself with our application criteria before submitting your project!

2. Choosing projects

We apply our criteria to find several projects that seem the most promising to us. If we find the project not applicable, we will let you know why we think so. In some cases we may politely suggest some changes, to make it more applicable.

3. Project prestudy

The projects we consider both worthwhile and having an acceptable risk to reward ratio we will invite to a prestudy. In a period of several weeks we will have multiple discussions with you where we refine the concept, consider technology, estimate risks, plan the project and schedule.

4. Project execution

For the projects that pass the prestudy phase with mutual acceptance by all the relevant parties, on we go with the design and implementation!
